Configure organization-wide notification policies in **Settings** > **Notifications**. Use tenant settings to establish default notification preferences for all users in your organization and control whether users can customize their own settings.

## Email notifications

Control whether C1 sends email notifications to users in your organization.

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Notifications**.
</Step>
<Step>
Toggle **Email notifications** on or off.
</Step>
</Steps>

When disabled, no users in your organization will receive email notifications. Slack and Microsoft Teams notifications are not affected.

## Default notification preferences

Set default notification preferences that apply to all users in your organization.

### Lock or unlock user customization

Choose whether users can modify their own notification preferences:

- **Unlocked** (default): New users receive the default settings but can customize their notification preferences.
- **Locked**: All users must use the default settings and cannot make changes.

When settings are locked, users can view their notification preferences but cannot edit them.

To lock or unlock notification settings:

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Notifications**.
</Step>
<Step>
Click the lock icon next to **User customization**.
</Step>
<Step>
Select **Locked** or **Unlocked**.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Digest email settings

Configure the default digest email preferences for your organization. Digest emails include task summaries and expiring access alerts.

**Digest frequency**

Choose how often users receive digest emails:

- **Daily**: Sent on weekdays at 9 AM Pacific time.
- **Weekly**: Sent on Tuesdays at 10 AM Pacific time.
- **Never**: Digest emails are disabled.

Digest emails are only sent if a user has at least one pending task or expiring access item.

To set the default digest frequency:

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Notifications**.
</Step>
<Step>
Under **Default preferences**, select **Digest frequency**.
</Step>
<Step>
Choose **Daily**, **Weekly**, or **Never**.
</Step>
<Step>
**Optional.** Click the lock icon to prevent users from changing this setting.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Notification types

Configure which types of notifications are enabled by default.

**Task summaries**

- Open tasks requiring action.
- Pending access requests awaiting approval.
- Access reviews assigned to users.
- Escalated requests.

**Access expiration alerts**

Notifications sent before a user's access to an application or resource expires.

**Admin notifications**

The following notifications are automatically enabled for admin users:

- **Connector sync issues**: Alerts when a connector sync encounters problems.
- **Resource deactivation**: Notifications when resources are deactivated in connected applications.

To configure default notification types:

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Notifications**.
</Step>
<Step>
Under **Default preferences**, toggle each notification type on or off.
</Step>
<Step>
**Optional.** Click the lock icon next to each type to prevent users from changing that setting.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Slack notifications

Once Slack is configured for your organization, Slack notification settings will appear in tenant settings.

To set up Slack for your organization, see [Set up the Slack app](/product/admin/slack-application).

After Slack is configured, you can:

- Set default Slack notification preferences for users.
- Lock or unlock Slack settings to control user customization.
- Configure which notification types are delivered via Slack by default.

## Microsoft Teams notifications

Once Microsoft Teams is configured for your organization, Teams notification settings will appear in tenant settings.

To set up Microsoft Teams for your organization, see [Set up the Microsoft Teams app](/product/admin/ms-teams-public).

After Teams is configured, you can:

- Set default Teams notification preferences for users.
- Lock or unlock Teams settings to control user customization.
- Configure which notification types are delivered via Teams by default.

## How users receive notifications

**New users** automatically receive the default notification preferences you configure, and can customize settings if user customization is unlocked.

**Existing users** keep their current settings when you change tenant defaults. If you lock a setting, their preferences are overridden by the tenant default. If you unlock a setting, they can modify their preferences again.

Users inherit tenant defaults in the following scenarios:

- When their account is created.
- When you lock a previously unlocked setting.
- When they click **Reset to defaults** in their notification preferences.

## Best practices

- **Start with unlocked settings**: Allow users to customize notifications based on their role and responsibilities.
- **Lock critical notifications**: Consider locking task alerts for access reviews.
- **Review quarterly**: Periodically review default settings to ensure they align with your organization's needs.
- **Communicate changes**: Notify users when you lock or unlock settings that affect their notification preferences.
