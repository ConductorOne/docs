## What can I do with the C1 Microsoft Teams app? 

Use C1's Microsoft Teams app to interact directly with C1 without leaving Microsoft Teams. Once the C1 Microsoft Teams app is installed for your workspace, you and your colleagues can: 

- Track your open requests for access and get notified about progress
- Get notified when an open access request needs your approval 
- Approve or deny access requests without leaving the Teams interface
- Receive notifications if a connector has a sync issue or a significant drop in synced resources
- Request new access without leaving the Teams interface

<Tip>
**Have a question? Need a hand?** 

[Contact our support team](mailto:support@c1.ai) for assistance with the C1 Teams app. 
</Tip>

## Set up the C1 MS Teams app for your organization

<Warning>
This task requires the **Admin** role in the Microsoft directory you're connecting and the **Super Administrator** role in C1.
</Warning>

### Step 1: Install the C1 app 

<Steps>
<Step>
In the Microsoft Teams app marketplace, navigate to the [C1 app](https://marketplace.microsoft.com/en-us/product/office/WA200009797) or search for "C1" and download the app. 
</Step>
</Steps>

#### Optional: Add the app to a Teams policy

If desired, you can add the C1 app to your organization's Teams interface by adding the app to a policy. 

<Steps>
<Step>
In the Microsoft Teams admin center, navigate to <b>Teams apps</b> &gt; <b>Manage apps</b>.
</Step>
<Step>
Search for the "C1" app in your app catalog.
</Step>
<Step>
Navigate to <b>Teams apps</b> &gt; <b>Setup policies</b>.
</Step>
<Step>
On the <b>Manage policies</b> tab, click the <b>Global (Org-wide default)</b> policy.<br/><br/>
If desired, you can create and use a custom policy here instead of the global policy.
</Step>
<Step>
On the policy's page, click <b>Add apps</b> and search for "C1".
</Step>
<Step>
Click <b>Select</b>, then click <b>Add</b> to add the C1 app to the policy.
</Step>
<Step>
<b>Optional.</b> Add the C1 app to the list of apps pinned to your organization's Teams sidebar.
</Step>
</Steps>


### Step 2: Connect the C1 Microsoft Teams app

Next, connect the C1 app for use in your organization's Microsoft Teams directory. 

<Steps>
<Step>
In C1, navigate to <b>Admin</b> &gt; <b>Settings</b> &gt; <b>Notifications</b>.
</Step>
<Step>
In the <b>Microsoft Teams</b> section of the page, click <b>Connect</b>.
</Step>
<Step>
You'll be directed to Microsoft Teams. Follow the prompts and review the permissions in Microsoft that the C1 app requires:
  <ul>
    <li>Organization.Read.All - used to fetch the tenant hostname</li>
    <li>User.Read.All - used to look up Teams users by email</li>
  </ul>
</Step>
</Steps>

**Done.** You'll be directed back to the C1 **Settings** page, where you'll see that your Microsoft Teams directory is now connected. 

<Tip>
**Need to connect to additional Teams directories?** 

No problem. Sign in to Microsoft Teams as an admin on the directory you want to connect and repeat the steps above. 
</Tip>

## Set up the C1 MS Teams app on an individual workstation

If a Teams admin at your organization has not automatically added the C1 app to your organization's Teams interface, you can set it up on your workstation yourself. Here's what to do: 

<Steps>
<Step>
In the Microsoft Teams app marketplace, navigate to the [C1 app](https://marketplace.microsoft.com/en-us/product/office/WA200009797) or search for "C1" and download the app. 

   If the app isn't available, check with an admin to ensure that they've completed the installation steps above.  

   If the app is available but you receive an error asking you to contact an admin, ensure they've completed the configuration process above. 
</Step>
</Steps>
**Done.** You're set up and ready to work with C1 from the comfort of your Teams interface. 

## Disconnect the C1 MS Teams app 

Fully disconnecting the C1 integration and removing the app is a two-step process. 

### Step 1: Disconnect from C1

<Steps>
<Step>
In C1, navigate to **Settings** > **Notifications**.
</Step>
<Step>
In the **Microsoft Teams** section of the page, find the Teams directory you want to disconnect and click **Remove**. 
</Step>
</Steps>

The integration between your C1 tenant and your Teams directory is now severed.  

### Step 2: Remove the Enterprise Application from Entra ID

<Warning>
This task requires the **Global Administrator** or **Application Administrator** role in Entra ID.  
</Warning>

<Steps>
<Step>
Log into the Entra ID Admin Center.
</Step>
<Step>
Navigate to <b>Enterprise Applications</b> &gt; <b>All applications</b>.
</Step>
<Step>
Find and select the "C1" app.
</Step>
<Step>
Click <b>Properties</b>, then select <b>Delete</b> and confirm your choice.
</Step>
</Steps>

**Done.** The app is removed from your Entra ID instance, and its Organization.Read.All and User.Read.All permissions have been fully disconnected from your C1 tenant.
