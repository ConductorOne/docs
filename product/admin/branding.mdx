</Warning>

Use branding settings to replace the default C1 logo, favicon, and display name with your own. Once branding is turned on, your assets appear in the C1 app header, the browser tab, the sign-in page, and email notifications sent to your users.

## What you can customize

Branding settings let you configure the following:

- **Display name.** The name shown alongside your logo in the app header and in email notifications. Maximum 128 characters.
- **Logo.** The primary logo shown on light backgrounds.
- **White logo.** An optional inverted version of your logo used on dark backgrounds. If you don't upload a white logo, C1 uses your primary logo everywhere.
- **Favicon / mobile logo.** The square icon shown in browser tabs, bookmarks, and on small mobile surfaces.
- **White favicon / mobile logo.** An optional inverted favicon for dark backgrounds. If you don't upload one, C1 uses your primary favicon everywhere.

## Turn on branding and set a display name

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Branding**.
</Step>
<Step>
In the **Branding settings** area of the page, click **Edit**.
</Step>
<Step>
Turn on the **Enable branding** toggle. When branding is off, C1 shows the default C1 logo, favicon, and name in the product and in emails.
</Step>
<Step>
In the **Display name** field, enter the name you want to appear alongside your logo. This name is used in the app header and in email notifications. Maximum 128 characters.
</Step>
<Step>
Click **Save**.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Upload a logo

Your logo appears in the C1 app header and in email notifications sent to your users.

**Requirements:**

- **File size:** 1 MB maximum
- **File format:** PNG, JPEG, or WebP (PNG preferred)
- **Aspect ratio:** No enforced ratio. A landscape ratio between 8:1 and 3:1 works best in the app header.

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Branding**.
</Step>
<Step>
In the **Logo** section, click **Upload** (or **Replace** if a logo is already set).
</Step>
<Step>
Drag an image into the upload area, or click to select a file from your computer.
</Step>
<Step>
Click **Save**. C1 automatically generates the resized versions needed for different screens and devices.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Optional: Upload a white logo

If your primary logo is dark and doesn't show up well on dark backgrounds, upload a white version. C1 uses the white logo in contexts where a dark background is displayed behind your logo.

<Steps>
<Step>
In the **White logo (optional)** section, click **Upload**.
</Step>
<Step>
Select a file that meets the same requirements as the primary logo.
</Step>
<Step>
Click **Save**.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Upload a favicon

Your favicon appears in the browser tab, in browser bookmarks, and as the mobile icon. Favicons must be square.

**Requirements:**

- **File size:** 512 KB maximum
- **File format:** PNG, JPEG, or WebP (PNG preferred)
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (square). You can crop a non-square source image when you upload it.

<Steps>
<Step>
Navigate to **Settings** > **Branding**.
</Step>
<Step>
In the **Favicon / mobile logo** section, click **Upload** (or **Replace** if a favicon is already set).
</Step>
<Step>
Drag an image into the upload area, or click to select a file from your computer.
</Step>
<Step>
Use the **Zoom** slider and drag the image inside the crop frame to position it. The image is cropped to a square.
</Step>
<Step>
Click **Save**. C1 automatically generates the resized versions needed for browsers and devices.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Optional: Upload a white favicon

Upload a white favicon if you want a different favicon on dark backgrounds.

<Steps>
<Step>
In the **White favicon / mobile logo (optional)** section, click **Upload**.
</Step>
<Step>
Select and crop a file that meets the same requirements as the primary favicon.
</Step>
<Step>
Click **Save**.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Replace or remove an asset

To swap an uploaded logo or favicon, click **Replace** on the asset and upload a new file. The new asset replaces the old one immediately after you save.

To stop using your custom branding without deleting your uploaded assets, turn off the **Enable branding** toggle. C1 reverts to default branding in the product and in emails, but keeps your uploaded files available to turn back on later.

## Where your branding appears

After you turn branding on and upload assets, your branding is applied in the following places:

- **App header.** Your logo and display name replace the default C1 logo in the top navigation.
- **Sign-in page.** Your logo appears on the C1 sign-in page for your tenant.
- **Browser tab and bookmarks.** Your favicon replaces the default C1 favicon.
- **Email notifications.** Your logo and display name appear in transactional emails, digest emails, and other notifications sent from C1.

Asset updates may take a few moments to propagate to all surfaces. Cached browser tabs may continue to show the previous favicon until the tab is refreshed.
